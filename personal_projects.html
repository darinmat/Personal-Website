Consumer Spending Insights

This project explores the influence of location, promotions, demographics, and loyalty on consumer buying behavior. By analyzing a dataset containing detailed information on consumer preferences, purchase history, and product choices, we aim to understand how these factors impact the frequency of purchases and basket size. The insights derived from this analysis provide businesses with practical strategies to tailor their marketing efforts, optimize sales processes, and enhance customer satisfaction and loyalty. Through visualizations and detailed findings, this project aims to guide companies in making informed decisions to drive sales and foster long-term engagement with their target audience.

From Ruin to Resilience

From my travels to Japan in the summer of 2024, I conducted both qualitative and quantitative research. on the disaster recovery process and land use in affected areas. Please read the abstract for my work here!

The population of Japan has faced a demographic and population crisis greatly influenced by the triple disasters of March 11, 2011. These include the Great East Japan Earthquake, subsequent tsunami and Fukushima-Daiichi nuclear meltdown that resulted in immense loss of lives, displacement of persons, and economic damages. The lengthy recovery period that followed demonstrated the dissatisfaction among affected communities that feel that there is nothing to return to. This research paper demonstrates how the effectiveness of urban planning and rebuilding strategies can determine whether or not populations are maintained in disaster-affected towns. It seeks to identify factors that enable some towns to better retain their populations through an examination of housing destruction rates, tourism development, population density, reconstruction timelines and location. The statistical analyses and case studies show that changes in housing destruction rates and population density are significant drivers of population changes while factors such as city tax revenue are less important due to equitable distribution of national government reconstruction funds as well as international aid. Consequently, this study's findings highlight the need for prioritization of residential and commercial reconstruction over disaster prevention infrastructure in order to minimize population decrease.

A Bettter Bettor - ELO Modeling NHL Teams

The NHL project focuses on the intersection of machine learning and sports betting, addressing how bettors can use artificial intelligence to make better, more rational decisions. The abstract outlines the significance of sports betting in the modern entertainment landscape and the challenges it poses due to irrational decision-making and addiction. It suggests that machine learning can enhance sports betting outcomes by analyzing large datasets to identify patterns and trends, offering more accurate and data-driven predictions compared to human intuition. The introduction provides context on the rapid growth of the sports betting industry in the U.S., driven by the legalization of betting in various states. It emphasizes the need for tools like machine learning to counterbalance the odds, which are generally tilted in favor of bookmakers. The methodology section details a two-step approach to predictive modeling: first, predicting game outcomes using the ELO Rating System to assess team strengths, and second, evaluating bookmakers’ odds to find profitable betting opportunities. The application of the Elo Rating System is explained in depth, noting its self-correcting nature and the Markov property, which allows predictions based on previous ratings. The model uses the difference in ELO ratings between teams to estimate expected goals, leveraging a Poisson distribution to predict actual goals scored. The conclusion focuses on comparing predictive models with bookmaker odds to identify value bets—those where the bettor’s implied probability is higher than the bookmaker’s estimate. This project illustrates how machine learning can empower bettors by reducing reliance on emotion and intuition, providing a more scientific approach to betting in hockey and, by extension, other sports. The project also highlights the complexities of translating odds into profitable bets, considering factors like vigorish and the bookmaker’s margin. Through this analysis, the project aims to contribute to a deeper understanding of how technology can shape the sports betting landscape, offering insights into potential future research directions and practical applications for bettors.

Technology in Sports

The project analyzing ticket scores in the Chase Arena for the Warriors with the NBA focused on optimizing ticket pricing strategies to maximize revenue and attendance. Using machine learning techniques, we developed a model to predict ticket scores, which are metrics that assess the quality and value of seating options based on factors like location, view, demand, and pricing elasticity. By analyzing historical ticket sales data, we identified key variables influencing ticket scores, such as opponent strength, day of the week, game type (regular season, playoffs), and ticket sales trends. The model incorporated these variables to forecast ticket scores and recommend dynamic pricing adjustments that could increase revenue while ensuring optimal fan experience. This project provided valuable insights for the NBA in understanding ticket market dynamics, enabling better decision-making for pricing strategies and helping the Warriors optimize ticket sales and fan engagement at home games. Through this analysis, we demonstrated how data science could be leveraged to enhance customer satisfaction and profitability in the sports entertainment sector.
